Blockchain, (originally called Block Chain), uses cryptology to link a series of blocks.
The blocks represent records of data.
The cryptology ensures that the data and information about the processed transactions remain secure.




Each transaction generates a hash.
Once a transation is approved by nodes, it is then written into a block.
Each block refers to the previous block created.
All of the blocks combined created a Blockchain.




Autonomous Things


"Autonomous things are things that work autonomously without human guidance or direct intervention." - Techopedia

Some examples of Autonomous Things are:
self-driven cars, drones and SmartHome Technologies.

There are obviously many advantages to autonomous things.
For example: Self driven cars could decrease the risk of drink driving. They would also decrease road accidents as most road accidents are caused by human error. Drones are becoming increasingly within the tourist industry as they help to create a more attractive image to potential tourists.

Of course with the advantages comes disadvantages.
For example: self driven cars would be extremely expensive for the average consumer. While drones are most definitely useful, they are easily prone to hackers, making them very unsecure.
